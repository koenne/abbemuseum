Van Abbe


Interrogation (what kind of bird are you?)

1956-1958 Max Ernst

In this predominantly red painting, Max Ernst uses black lines to depict the contours of a large and a small figure. Furthermore, the work is made up of a lot of small angular shapes. These shapes, which appear to partially overlap, were created by putting paint on the canvas or scraping it off again with a palette knife. Ernst may also have placed pieces of smooth material of a certain thickness, for example cardboard, behind the canvas while working. The shapes of these then penetrate into the painting. In the bottom right corner is the word INTERROGATION. In the middle of the bottom edge is the sentence WHAT KIND OF A BIRD ARE YOU? upside down.
