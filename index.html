Van Abbemuseum

The Van Abbemuseum is a museum for modern and contemporary art in the center of the North Brabant city of Eindhoven. The museum opened in 1936. The Van Abbe Museum is named after its founder Henri van Abbe. Van Abbe was a lover of modern art and wanted Eindhoven to enjoy it. As of 2010, the museum's collection contains more than 2,700 works of art, including approximately 1,000 works on paper, 700 paintings and 1,000 sculptures, installations and video works. The museum is in possession of one of the largest collections of works of art by the Russian El Lissitzky in the world. It also features works by Pablo Picasso and Wassily Kandinsky.
